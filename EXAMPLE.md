# Example run — a real channel check, unedited

Test idea, checked live on **2026-07-14** with nothing but web search and web
fetch (no API keys, no paid tools):

> **"A note-taking app built around the #1 complaint in a popular note app's
> one-star reviews."**

## Step 0 — Buyer and search moment

First, find the complaint. Cross-checking 2026 review roundups and low-star
review analyses: **Notion's most persistent low-star complaint is the lack of a
true offline mode** — cloud-first, locked out of your notes on a flight or an
outage. (Evernote's one-star pile is bigger but scattered: sync data loss, the
$59→$249 pricing hike, the Electron-rebuild performance collapse. Notion's is
one clean, recurring wound.)

So the derived idea is an **offline-first note app for Notion refugees**, and:

- **BUYER:** a Notion power user burned by the spinner on a plane/outage
- **SEARCH MOMENT:** types **"Notion offline alternative"** / **"offline notes"**
  into Google, YouTube, or the App Store

## Step 2 — Live checks (what actually came back)

**App Store search** — Apple's free search API for `offline notes`:
Standard Notes (4.67★, 3,647 ratings), Obsidian (4.49★, 2,610), Google Keep
(4.59★, 28,970), Goodnotes (4.72★, 434,772), Apple Notes (4.84★, 611,916).
The keyword is stocked top to bottom, and the two apps positioned EXACTLY as
"offline/private notes" — Obsidian and Standard Notes — are free.

**Google SEO** — SERP for `best offline note taking app`: one Zapier listicle,
then small indie sites — including **two indie note apps ranking their own blog
posts** (LocalOneLabs, Memory Chat) in the top 8. Low-authority sites rank this
long tail today, and incumbents are already running the
write-the-comparison-post play.

**Reddit** — direct reddit.com fetches are 403-blocked (as the skill warns);
search-snippet stats sites quoted 57K/346K, but the live sidebars (verified
in-browser 2026-07-14) say **r/NoteTaking = 21K members** (350 weekly
contributions) and **r/Notion = 115K members** (1K weekly) — third-party stats
ran up to 3x stale, which is exactly why the skill grades on what the page
shows today. Still the room where the offline complaint recurs, reachable by a
human founder with a genuine build log; both subs police drive-by self-promo.

**YouTube search** — `offline note taking app`: category listicles pull
110K–194K views (Mike and Matty 113K, Techlore 194K, None The Wiser 110K). The
exact intersection is nearly unserved: "Best Notion Alternative For Offline
Users 2026" has **738 views**; "AppFlowy vs AFFiNE" (open-source Notion clones)
has 5,864. "Best Notion Alternatives" by Tool Finder: 56,974 — the intent
exists; the specific answer video doesn't.

**Instagram/TikTok** — the #notetaking tag page loads through a reader proxy but
shows **no aggregate view counts** (TikTok removed them). Demand there is
unmeasurable pre-build. Skipped X for the same reason: login-walled search.

**Product Hunt** — notes category is active; local-first launches (e.g. Bamboo)
get featured; 100+ upvotes = a "successful" launch in 2026 benchmarks. A
launch-day spike, not a channel.

Note what happened: the idea SURVIVED demand validation (the pain is real,
recurring, documented in thousands of reviews) and still nearly died at
distribution — 3 of 6 channels closed, and the winnable one isn't the one a
founder would have picked. That's the whole point. **Validate the channel, not
the idea.**
